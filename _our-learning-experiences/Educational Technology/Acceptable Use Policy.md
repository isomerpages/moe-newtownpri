<p><strong>Purpose of the Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) for use of EdTech Resources</strong>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Technology is an essential part of learning in today’s world.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>
<p>This Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) establishes guidelines on the appropriate
use of EdTech resources to help students understand how to use these resources
safely and responsibly, in line with efforts to develop their digital literacies
and growth as digitally responsible citizens.&nbsp;</p>
<p>EdTech resources include the following, but are not limited to:&nbsp;</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Learning devices (e.g. school devices)&nbsp;</p>
</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>
<p>ICT system accounts (e.g. Singapore Student Learning Space (SLS), iCON);
and&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>school internet networks.&nbsp;</p>
<p>For more information, please refer to:&nbsp;</p>
<p>Student Kit on Cyber Wellness and Learning with a Learning Device (Primary)
– <a href="https://go.gov.sg/cw-studentkitpri" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank"><u>https://go.gov.sg/cw-studentkitpri</u></a>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>General</strong>&nbsp;</p>
<ol>
<li>
<p>Students should not attempt to access data, system and information that
they are not authorised to.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>
</li>
</ol>
<ol start="2">
<li>
<p>Students are reminded that the use of learning devices and school’s EdTech
resources should solely be for the purpose of learning.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>
</li>
</ol>
<ol start="3">
<li>
<p>Students are expected to handle the school’s learning devices with care.
Students are responsible for any damage or loss caused to the devices or
accessories due to poor handling or misuse and will bear the cost of repair
or replacement.&nbsp;</p>
</li>
</ol>
<p><strong>Being a Responsible Digital Citizen</strong>&nbsp;</p>
<ol start="4">
<li>
<p>Students should interact with others in a respectful and responsible way.
Students should not post online remarks that are&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>
</li>
</ol>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>racially and/or religiously insensitive,&nbsp;</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>vulgar and/or offensive, or&nbsp;</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>hurtful to others.&nbsp;</p>
</li>
</ul>
<ol start="5">
<li>
<p>Students should not use any devices to&nbsp;</p>
</li>
</ol>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>store, modify, create or share content (e.g. documents, presentations,
pictures and videos) that is inappropriate (e.g. indecent, pornographic,
defamatory, hurtful to self or others).&nbsp;</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>make threats, cause harassment or embarrassment, impersonate or intimidate
others.&nbsp;</p>
</li>
</ul>
<ol start="6">
<li>
<p>Students should not use MOE/school-deployed ICT system accounts for any
commercial activities (e.g. buying and selling of goods and services).&nbsp;</p>
</li>
</ol>
<p>Non-compliance with the above will lead to disciplinary action in accordance
with the school’s discipline policy.&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>Respecting Copyright&nbsp;</strong>&nbsp;</p>
<ol start="7">
<li>
<p>Students are reminded to obtain explicit permission from the content owner(s)
before downloading, copying or sharing any copyrighted materials (e.g.
pictures, videos, music).&nbsp;</p>
</li>
</ol>
<ol start="8">
<li>
<p>Students should not use, copy, edit or share digital files in an unauthorised
or illegal manner.&nbsp;</p>
</li>
</ol>
<p><strong>Using Artificial Intelligence (AI)</strong>&nbsp;</p>
<ol start="9">
<li>
<p>Students must ensure that students meet the minimum age requirement specified
in each AI tool's age restrictions before using it.&nbsp;</p>
</li>
</ol>
<ol start="10">
<li>
<p>If the use of AI is permitted, students should acknowledge the use of
AI in weighted assessments and homework as required.&nbsp;</p>
</li>
</ol>
<ol start="11">
<li>
<p>Students are to practice academic integrity and be responsible for their
own learning when using AI. Understand that students are ultimately short-changing
themselves if students pass off other’s work as their own.&nbsp;</p>
</li>
</ol>
<p><strong>Staying Safe and Secure online</strong>&nbsp;</p>
<ol start="12">
<li>
<p>Students should report any incidents (e.g. unusual device behaviour or
inappropriate use of devices), to the school.&nbsp;</p>
</li>
</ol>
<ol start="13">
<li>
<p>Students are reminded to develop online safety habits. This includes not
disclosing personal access credentials (e.g. MIMS password, PLD passcode,
etc.), sensitive personal data (e.g. home address, passwords) online or
on AI platforms, verifying credibility of online content before sharing,
avoiding clicking on suspicious links or downloading unknown files, and
being cautious when interacting with others online (e.g. on social media)
by not engaging with strangers.&nbsp;</p>
</li>
</ol>
<ol start="14">
<li>
<p>Students should exercise caution regarding the limitations of AI tools,
including potential inaccuracies / fabricated responses, inherent biases
and outdated information.&nbsp;</p>
</li>
</ol>
<p><strong>Digital Wellbeing and Balance</strong>&nbsp;</p>
<ol start="15">
<li>
<p>Students are reminded to balance screentime with other activities including
physical exercise and face-to-face social interactions.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>
</li>
</ol>
<ol start="16">
<li>
<p>Students should avoid excessive use of their devices outside learning
hours.&nbsp;</p>
</li>
</ol>
<ol start="17">
<li>
<p>Students should take regular breaks to rest their eyes and mind.&nbsp;</p>
</li>
</ol>
<ol start="18">
<li>
<p>Students are reminded to practice good sleep hygiene by not using their
devices one hour before bedtime.&nbsp;</p>
</li>
</ol>
