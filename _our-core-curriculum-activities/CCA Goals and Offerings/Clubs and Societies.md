<h3>Clubs and Societies</h3>
<p>Clubs &amp; Societies CCAs allow students to explore and extend their
interests in both wide-ranging and specialised areas which may be knowledge-based
or skills-based. Students are honed in information, communication and technical
skills as they strive to grow their mastery of the specialised areas.</p>
<p>We offer the following Clubs &amp; Societies CCAs:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Art and Crafts</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Robotics Club</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Speech &amp; Drama</p>
</li>
</ul>
<h4><strong>Art &amp; Crafts</strong></h4>
<p>The Art &amp; Crafts club caters to students who are passionate about
visual art and crafts and want to pursue their interests to deepen their
knowledge, skills and understanding of Art. Members have the opportunity
to use different mediums and work with both 2-dimensional and 3-dimensional
art forms. They interact and collaborate with like-minded peers and teachers-in-charge
to expand their creativity, ideas and thoughts. Members can take part in
the biennial Singapore Youth Festival (SYF) Arts Exhibition, a national
platform to communicate, showcase and benchmark their creativity and ideas.</p>

<h4><strong>Robotics Club</strong></h4>
<p>Members of Robotics CCA develop construction and programming skills to
build robots that perform specific tasks. They are provided with opportunities
for collaborative learning, enabling them to strengthen their teamwork
and problem-solving abilities. In-house competitions are organized to allow
members to challenge themselves and showcase their skills. Dedicated members
who demonstrate both aptitude and a positive attitude during CCA sessions
are selected to represent the school in external competitions.&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>Objectives</strong>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Robotics members will be developed holistically across three key areas:&nbsp;</p>
<p>1. Skills:&nbsp;</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Problem-solving and critical thinking skills through programming, building,
and troubleshooting robots.&nbsp;</p>
</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Creativity and innovation in designing solutions to real-world challenges.&nbsp;</p>
</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Technical skills in coding, engineering design, and use of robotics tools
and software.&nbsp;</p>
</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Collaboration and communication skills when working in teams to complete
projects and competitions.&nbsp;</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>2. Values:&nbsp;</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Perseverance and resilience in overcoming setbacks and failures.&nbsp;</p>
</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Responsibility in handling equipment and completing assigned tasks.&nbsp;</p>
</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Integrity and sportsmanship during competitions and teamwork.&nbsp;</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>3. Leadership:&nbsp;</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Taking initiative to guide and support peers in projects and competitions.&nbsp;</p>
</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Developing decision-making and project management skills.&nbsp;</p>
</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Fostering teamwork by encouraging inclusiveness and respect for diverse
ideas.&nbsp;</p>
</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>Programme Highlights</strong>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong><u>Basic Group</u></strong>&nbsp;</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p><strong>Hands-on Building:</strong> Develop basic construction techniques
to assemble functional robots.&nbsp;</p>
</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>
<p><strong>Computational Thinking and Coding:</strong> Learn fundamental programming
skills to control robot movements effectively.&nbsp;</p>
</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>
<p><strong>Teamwork and Communication:</strong> Collaborate respectfully with
teammates to achieve shared goals.&nbsp;</p>
</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>
<p><strong>Problem-Solving:</strong> Apply learning in practical and engaging
ways through in-house challenges.&nbsp;</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p><strong><u>Advanced Group</u></strong>&nbsp;</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p><strong>Research and Information Literacy:</strong> Conduct online research
on different robot designs to identify those most suitable for various
missions.&nbsp;</p>
</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>
<p><strong>Critical Thinking and Analytical Skills:</strong> Compare and analyse
different robots to determine the most effective design.&nbsp;</p>
</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>
<p><strong>Competitions and Showcases:</strong> Represent the school in national
robotics competitions, applying skills and showcasing creativity.&nbsp;</p>
</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>
<p><strong>Leadership and Collaboration:</strong> Take on leadership roles
within the CCA by guiding junior members, sharing knowledge, and demonstrating
initiative during teamwork.&nbsp;</p>
</li>
</ul>

<h4><strong>Speech and Drama</strong></h4>
<p>Speech and Drama CCA believes in building confidence and creativity through
performance. Students learn skills such as public speaking, voice projection,
expression, and stage presence while exploring drama techniques like role-play,
improvisation, and script work. At New Town Primary School’s Speech and
Drama Club, we aim to foster students’ teamwork, develop their imagination
and grow their love for the performing arts.&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>Objectives</strong>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Speech and Drama students will be developed in these five key areas:&nbsp;</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Communicate clearly and effectively, focusing on clear articulation, voice
modulation, and vocal projection.&nbsp;</p>
</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Express emotions and ideas through various forms of presentation and drama
performance to enhance their creativity and imagination.&nbsp;</p>
</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Work collaboratively in a group for presentation and ensemble for drama
performances, emphasizing the importance of cooperation and active listening.&nbsp;</p>
</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Learn and apply key drama techniques such as improvisation, character
building, and script interpretation, stagecraft and scriptwriting in service
of enhancing performance, for example prop-making, sets and costumes.&nbsp;</p>
</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Build confidence by providing opportunities for public speaking, performance
and other drama related activities (e.g. prop-making).&nbsp;</p>
</li>
</ul>
